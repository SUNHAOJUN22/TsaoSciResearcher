# Research paradigms

Classify the work before selecting methods.

| Paradigm | Core question | Minimum validity condition |
|---|---|---|
| Descriptive | What is present or how does it vary? | Representative measurement and transparent scope |
| Explanatory | Why does the pattern occur? | Mechanism or competing explanations are tested |
| Predictive | What will occur for new cases? | Out-of-sample validation and applicability domain |
| Causal | What changes because of an intervention? | Identification assumptions and counterfactual design |
| Design | Which structure/process meets objectives? | Explicit objectives, constraints and validation |
| Mechanistic | Which process links cause and outcome? | Discriminating evidence across candidate mechanisms |

A research question is operational only when it identifies the object, variables, boundary conditions, outcome, comparison, evidence route and falsification criterion. Broad topics remain in scoping mode.
